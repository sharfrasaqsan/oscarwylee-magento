What are aviator sunglasses?

Aviator sunglasses are a type of sunglasses, dating back to the 1930s. First created for US pilots to protect their eyes in the air, aviator sunglasses emphasise functionality. Their tear-drop lenses and wide facial and eye coverage, aim to block out as much sunlight and glare as possible.

Aviator sunglasses have also been an iconic long lasting fashion trend, embodying a certain swagger and cool vibe. Aviator sunglasses have been popularised by pop culture icons such as Freddie Mercury, Stevie Nicks and Lenny Kravitz. Today, aviator style sunglasses remain a fashion staple for many.

Aviator sunglasses were originally designed with a green lens and plastic frame, then later a metal frame. Why are they called aviator sunglasses?

The first aviator style sunglasses were invented to protect a pilot’s eyes whilst flying- hence the name aviator. In the 1930s, US pilots were flying to new, impressive heights. With this advancement however, came trouble with headaches, nausea and glare. The US eye health firm Bausch & Lomb, created aviator sunglasses to combat these issues. The design of the sunglasses resembles pilot’s goggles, with the lense covering as much as the eye and surrounding area as possible. This allows the sunglasses to give the wearer high protection from sunlight and glare.

The popularity of the classic aviator sunglasses style

In 1937, a year after being created for military use, aviator sunglasses were released to the public. According to the BBC, military wear was hugely influential on fashion trends in the 30s, and the potential of these sunglasses as a fashion accessory became quickly apparent.

Source: BBC

In the proceeding years, aviator sunglasses were popularised by many influential stars including Elvis Presley, Freddie Mercury and Tom Cruise in Top Gun. Today the glasses remain popular, often seen on high profile runways and in fashion magazines. Their fashion-forward yet practical design have also made them a popular street-wear staple.

Are aviator sunglasses unisex?

Aviator sunglasses are a unisex product, suitable for women and men. Though they may have a more masculine-orientated origin, many famous women such as Stevie Nicks, Gloria Steinem and more recently Jennifer Lopez, have popularised the aviator design for women. Genderfluid fashion icons such as David Bowie have also worn them, showing that aviator sunglasses are perfect for any gender.

What face shape suits aviator sunglasses?

Aviator sunglasses are traditionally more suitable for oval or triangular face shapes. However, with a variety of designs available, you may find something that suits you outside of these face shapes. How to clean aviator sunglasses

The cleaning process for your aviator sunglasses will be the same as it would be for any other pair of glasses. Use lens spray and a microfibre cloth to clean your sunglasses lenses, gently removing any smudge marks. You can use our lens spray available in store.

It is not recommended to use your breath or clothes to clean glasses as this may be abrasive and scratch the lens.
