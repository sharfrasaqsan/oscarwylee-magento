How do Blue Light-Blocking Glasses Work?

Blue light blocking glasses work by reducing your exposure to the potentially harmful artificial blue light by blocking these rays produced by screens. You can wear them when doing computer work, spending hours gaming or simply when on your phone.

While blue light blocking glasses are effective at filtering artificial blue light emitted from digital screens, research is still early to conclusively say they are successful in shielding your eyes from harm. Despite this, there is substantial evidence indicating that wearing blue light glasses is beneficial for sleep regulation. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), exposure to blue light can make it difficult to fall and stay asleep by affecting your circadian rhythm.

According to one of our Oscar Wylee optometrists, looking through blue light glasses can make lights appear dimmer, filtering out excess light that helps with symptoms, especially to those who are generally light-sensitive.

What are the Common Features of the Best Blue Light-Blocking Glasses?

The common features of the best blue light-blocking glasses include a blue light filter, an anti-glare coating, a small amount of magnification, a tinted lens for colour correction and a lightweight frame. The reasons these features are beneficial in blue light glasses are listed below.

  1. Blue Light Filter: This is a coating that is added to the lens or is built into the lens of a pair of glasses to block artificial blue light passing through to your eyes.
  2. Anti-glare coating: This reflective coating is an extremely thin layer placed on the lens that removes any reflections you might normally see from the surface of your glasses.
  3. Magnification/prescription: A small amount of magnification can prevent squinting. This is especially beneficial for people who spend much of their time gaming.
  4. Tinted lens: Blue light glasses can have an amber or yellow tint added to the lens which offers an extra buffer against blue light.
  5. Lightweight frame: For people who spent long periods of time in front of a screen, it is essential to have blue light glasses that are comfortable so you can wear them all day long.

Are Blue Light Blocking Glasses Used for Computers?

Yes, blue light blocking glasses are used when on a computer as they block the artificial light that is emitted from the screen. Computer glasses often include an intermediate prescription to help you see a computer screen more clearly and comfortably. They will always have an anti-reflective coating applied to them as well as a blue light filter to help further reduce potential eye strain induced by blue light.

Are Blue Light Blocking Glasses Used for the TV?

Yes, blue light-blocking glasses can be used when watching TV. Your television emits artificial blue light just as your computer or phone screen does. The majority of modern televisions will have an in-built blue light filter which can be switched on and off when you desire. While it is not always possible to limit your TV time, wearing blue light glasses can help with this issue.

What is the Difference Between Sunglasses and Blue Light-blocking Glasses?

The main difference between sunglasses and blue light-blocking glasses is that they are designed for different purposes. Polarised sunglasses offer UV protection and also cut out glare but they do not block or filter blue light rays. Blue light glasses, on the other hand, are specifically designed to block the artificial blue light that is emitted from digital screens.
