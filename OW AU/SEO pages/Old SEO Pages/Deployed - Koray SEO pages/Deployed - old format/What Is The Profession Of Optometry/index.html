What Is The Profession Of Optometry?

Optometry is a profession involving eye health and is practiced by optometrists. These eye care professionals are the primary contact for eye-related services.

Keep reading to find out more about the profession of optometry.

What is optometry?

Optometry is a health care profession that specialises in eye examination and related areas such as eye conditions and disorders. Optometrists practice in the profession of optometry and their typical services include comprehensive eye examinations and providing prescriptions.

What does an optometrist do?

An optometrist provides comprehensive eye examinations relating to both visual concerns regarding ocular surface diseases and prescription errors. These eye care professionals are your first point of contact in your eye care journey.

What services does an optometrist provide?

Optometrists in Australia commonly provide the following services:

  • Complete eye exams
  • Identifies and manages vision problems and eye infections
  • Prescribes eyeglasses and contact lenses
  • Diagnoses and treats eye disorders
  • Provides advice on eye care to each patient
  • Refers patients to ophthalmologists as needed

Is an optometrist a doctor?

No, optometrists are not medical doctors as they have not attended medical school like ophthalmologists. Read more about the differences between these eye care professionals.

What is the difference between optometry and ophthalmology?

The profession of ophthalmology is different from optometry because it requires a medical degree as ophthalmologists perform surgery.

Optometry deals with primary eye care and conditions and anything that is outside the scope of an optometrist, the patient is then referred to an ophthalmologist.

Where does an optometrist practice?

Optometrists work in but are not limited to private practice. They also practice optometry in various health settings such as hospitals, community and government organisations and in low vision clinics.

How are optometrists qualified?

In order to become a qualified optometrist, you need to complete a university degree in Optometry that is recognised by OCANZ (Optometry Council of Australia and New Zealand). You are then registered to AHPRA (Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency).

What are the minimum requirements for optometry?

The minimum requirement to enter a career in optometry is a completed university degree over a 3.5-7 year period, depending on your university.

Source: Optometry Australia

What can you do with an optometry degree?

Once you have completed a degree in Optometry, there are many career pathways you can take including:

  • Working in a clinical practice
  • Working in a hospital or community health centre
  • Owning your own optometry business
  • Working as a clinical teacher at a university
  • Entering vision research or academia
  • Participating in local and international eye outreach programs
Source: Optometry Australia

When should I see an optometrist?

We recommend that you see an optometrist for a routine eye test every 1-2 years. If you are over the age of 65 years old, you should book an eye test annually.

You should also visit an optometrist if you need:

  • A comprehensive eye examination
  • Assessment of an eye injury
  • A vision test or screening
  • Follow-up care after eye surgery once discharged by your ophthalmologist

Or, if you notice any significant or sudden change in your eyesight, seek care from an eye care professional immediately.
