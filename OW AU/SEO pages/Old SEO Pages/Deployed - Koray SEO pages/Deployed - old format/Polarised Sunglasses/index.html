How do polarised lenses work?

Polarised lenses work by filtering out half of the wavelength of light that reaches the glasses. This half that was filtered away, the horizontal wave of light, is the half that contained glare, reflections, and intense brightness. As the glasses have now blocked this layer of light, you can now see clearer and glares cannot reach your eyes.

This effect is achieved by how the lenses are created, and by how the tint applies to the glasses.

Creating polarised lenses

Polarised lenses are made most commonly by taking a soft PVA material that is heated and stretched to form a long chain molecule. This long chain molecule is vertically aligned along the lense in one direction, like blinds on a window. This sheet is then laminated between 2 high quality lenses using an injection mold.

The ‘window blinds’ make it so that only polarised vertical light may reach your eyes. This method is especially common for Oakley products and other brands, according to Dave Barton.

A common misconception about polarised sunglasses is that there is a ‘special chemical’ involved in the creation. This chemical is called silver halide, and it is used in transition lenses, not polarisation.

How to tell if your sunglasses are polarised

If you don’t know if your sunnies are polarised, take them off, rotate them 90 degrees and put them in front of your computer or phone screen. If the screen darkens, changes colour or disappears completely, then you have polarised sunglasses.

Alternative test for polarised lenses

If you have 2 pairs of polarised sunglasses, you can perform an even more accurate test. Hold one pair of glasses in front of the other, then rotate one 90 degrees. If the lens darkens or turns black, the lens is polarised.

Polarised lenses and UV blocking

Although it’s easy to get them confused, polarisation and UV blocking are not the same thing. Polarised lenses are a type of glass with filters that only let in a certain type of light. Your favourite brands UV blocking glasses are simply made of a material or have a coating applied that blocks UV rays.

How to clean polarised sunglasses

No matter what cleaning method you use, be very careful not to scratch or damage the lenses. You can clean your sunglasses with water and a mild detergent, a gentle soap or rubbing alcohol and a clean, gentle cloth. If any of these cleaning products are too strong, your polarised sunglasses may start showing signs of wear and tear much earlier.

Source: Precision Eye Care

Will water damage my polarised lenses?

According to Safety gear pro, water will not typically damage your sunnies. However, if they are submerged for a long time, they will eventually be affected. This damage will happen faster with salt or chlorine water.

Source: Safety Gear Pro
