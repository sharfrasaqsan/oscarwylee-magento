What is an eye test?

An eye test is a procedure in which an optometrist will check your eyes to assess your vision. Eye tests are hugely important in helping to maintain your eye health, as they can detect a wide range of vision issues and eye diseases. These can include:

  • Myopia (nearsightedness) and hyperopia (farsightedness).
  • Astigmatism.
  • Glaucoma.
  • Cataracts

Getting a regular eye test will allow your optometrist to diagnose and treat these conditions early on, so they do not become a bigger problem down the track. Even if you feel like your vision is clear, it is important to get your eyes tested regularly.

What can an eye test tell me about my health?

here are several health conditions that present symptoms within the eye, including diabetes and high blood pressure. This means that having a regular eye test may be able to help detect these issues and give an indication of your general health. Other health conditions that may be detected in an eye test include:

  • Cardiovascular disease.
  • Thyroid disease.
  • Rheumatoid arthritis.

Whilst a routine eye test may pick these conditions up, it is always best to speak with your doctor if you believe you have any health issues.

What is included in your comprehensive eye test?

At Oscar Wylee, your comprehensive eye exam will include a variety of tests to ensure we can diagnose and treat any vision issues appropriately. You will first undergo a series of pre-tests, sometimes called diagnostic tests, with an optical assistant. This may include the following:

  • Tonometry test - during this test, a machine will blow small puffs of air into your eyes. This test will measure intraocular eye pressure.
  • A digital photograph may be taken of your eye, which can be used in later appointments to assess how your eye is changing.

Our optometrists will then conduct their own tests, these may include:

  • Slit lamp test- this test assesses your eye health, by examining the anterior and posterior of the eye such as your cornea and iris.
  • Refraction or vision test- during this test, an optometrist will have you look at an image through different lenses, asking you to decide which helps you see clearer.

There are some additional tests that will not be included in your regular exam but may be conducted at an optometrist's discretion.

Types of additional eye tests

The following eye tests may be performed alongside your regular comprehensive eye test, if an optometrist needs a more complete picture of how your eyes are functioning.

  • Visual field test - This test is used to assess your peripheral vision. It is particularly useful for detecting glaucoma, which is a condition that can cause vision loss
  • Dilated Fundus Exam - This test enables the optometrist to evaluate your eye health in more detail, by obtaining a clear view of the retina. This test is normally done for those with diabetes and those at risk of retinal damage, such as tears and holes.

What happens during an eye test?

During our eye tests at Oscar Wylee, we undertake a series of simple, time efficient steps to complete your examination.

  1. You need to first book an eye test with us. You can do this online or visit your nearest store to make an appointment. Fill out your details correctly and make sure to bring your old glasses, medicare or health fund card if you have them.
  2. When you arrive, you will be checked in and asked for your medical history, family medical history, why you are here, what concerns you have, any symptoms and any existing prescriptions.
  3. Before you see your optometrist, you will complete pre-testing with one of our optical assistants.
  4. Once finished with these diagnostic tests, you will see an optometrist. The optometrist will conduct their own tests.
  5. When all the tests have been completed, your optometrist will discuss your results with you, and make recommendations for prescriptions or further treatment if needed.
  6. Our friendly staff will then take over to discuss lens options and help you choose a pair of glasses. Dispensing for new glasses often takes 2-3 weeks.

How often do I need an eye test?

If you are under 65 years old, it is recommended that you have a routine eye test every 2 years. If you are over 65 years old, then a yearly review is ideal. If you have a condition like diabetes, you will likely need eye tests more frequently.

You may also need an eye test if you have noticed any vision changes. These can include:

  • Blurred, double or cloudy vision.
  • Eye strain.
  • Squinting.
  • Headaches or migraines.
  • Sore eyes.
  • Seeing spots.

Do children need to get an eye test?

Being able to see clearly is crucial for a child’s development. It can impact how they succeed in areas such as reading and writing. It is therefore hugely important that you get your child’s eyes tested, so any vision problems can be detected and treated early.

Source: Optometrists Network

When to get my child’s eyes tested?

It is recommended that you get your child’s eyes tested before starting school, if you think they have vision problems or at the suggestion of an optometrist.

Source: NSW Health

It is important to note that despite these recommendations, not all optometrists will provide eye tests to children. It is crucial that you check with an optometrist before making any appointments.

Can I test my eyesight at home?

It is possible to test your eyesight at home, using an online test or printed eye chart, such as a Snellen Chart. However, home eye tests are not always accurate and the results can be misleading.

If you feel like you are having vision problems and need an eye test, it is important that you book an appointment with an optometrist for accurate results and appropriate treatment.
