What is bulk billing Medicare?

Medicare is Australia’s healthcare system. The service was officially started in 1984. Since then, it has provided coverage and gap payments for Australian citizens and permanent residents for all payments going towards health costs and medical aid. Services Australia are currently managing it.

Bulk billing is when a healthcare specialist sends all the bills for their services that are covered by Medicare to the government at once, to be paid in one instance.

How often should you get your eyes checked?

It is recommended that you check your eyes every 2 years, even if you have not noticed any changes in your vision. This is because very early changes in your eye health may not lead to any changes in your eyesight, and may only be detected through an eye test. You may also be suffering from undetected eye strain and vision problems.

If you have noticed any changes in your vision, or have medical conditions like diabetes, or are at risk of eye conditions like glaucoma or macular degeneration, a more frequent eye examination may be recommended by your optometrist.

Does Medicare cover laser eye surgery?

In Australia, Medicare’s rebates only cover for a selection of optometrists services. As such, laser eye surgery or other intensive optical correction that requires a private party’s involvement is not included as part of Medicare’s coverage.

Full list of which services have Medicare rebates available can be found on the Medicare Benefits Schedule.

Does Medicare cover glasses?

Government Medicare services will not cover the cost of glasses, contact lenses, corrective eye surgery or other optical procedures.

Claiming with health funds

While Medicare might not cover for the cost of new prescription glasses, most private health funds have an option for covering glasses as part of a specific policy. This type of coverage is called optical extras and they can be used at optical stores and enable no gap purchases on many optical appliances.
