5 Benefits Of Hand-Eye Coordination

Content

  • Performance Enhancement in Sporting Competition: Performance enhancement in sporting competitions can be a benefit of having good eye-hand coordination as it allows people to respond quicker when performing actions such as catching and throwing balls, as well as help with accuracy.
  • Better Academic Performance: Better academic performance can be one benefit of good hand-eye coordination, particularly for children in areas such as mathematics.
  • Reduced Risk of Falls and Injuries: A reduced risk of falls and injuries may be a benefit of good hand-eye coordination as it can make people less clumsy and quicker in reaction times.
  • Improved Social Skills: Improved social skills for kids is a possible benefit of having good eye-hand coordination as it may help kids interact better with parents, in turn developing good communication and social skills.
  • Improved Development of Children: Improved development in children is another benefit of having good hand-eye coordination skills as it assists in helping children learn how to communicate and to do basic tasks such as reading, writing, tying shoe laces and doing up zippers.

How to Improve Hand-Eye Coordination?

Content

  • Playing Catch: Playing catch may help improve hand-eye coordination as it requires the hands and eyes to work together to catch and throw a ball.
  • Knitting: Knitting may help improve hand-eye coordination as it requires concentration and fine motor skills to coordinate the hands and eyes to move the needles.
  • Drawing and Writing: Drawing and writing may help improve hand-eye coordination as it requires the hands and eyes to work together to move the pen or pencil across the paper.
  • Playing Video Games: Playing video games may help improve hand-eye coordination, according to a 2016 study by Li Li, Rongrong Chen, and Jing Chen. This study tested the hand-eye coordination of non-gamers to those who did play video games, noting that gamers had quicker reaction times and better accuracy skills.
  • Taking Up a Sport: Taking up a sport, particularly a ball-based sport like tennis or basketball may also improve hand-eye coordination, as it requires the hands and eyes to work together to track, catch and throw or hit a ball.
