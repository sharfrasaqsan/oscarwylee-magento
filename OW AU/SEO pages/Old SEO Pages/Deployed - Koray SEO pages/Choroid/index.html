What are the Eye Diseases that Affect the Choroid?

Content

  • Chorioretinitis: Chorioretinitis is a type of posterior uveitis that causes inflammation of the choroid and retina and can cause symptoms such as floaters, flashing lights and loss of vision. Causes of chorioretinitis can be categorised as infectious and non-infectious. Infectious causes include tuberculosis and syphilis and non-infectious causes include eye injuries and rheumatoid arthritis.
