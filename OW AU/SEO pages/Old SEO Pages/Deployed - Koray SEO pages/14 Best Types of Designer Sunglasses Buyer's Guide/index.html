How to Choose the Best Designer Glasses?

The best designer glasses can be chosen by using the following methods: finding the right glasses style for the wearer’s face shape, carefully selecting the frames size to fit the wearer’s face, choosing pair of glasses that best match the wearers purpose and choosing the material that will suit the purpose the wearer wants. The following steps should be followed when choosing the best designer glasses.

  1. Find the right glasses style for the face shape: A person's face shape must be clear and defined before a wearer decides on the right pair of glasses. An oval face shape, for instance, will look better in sharper and blockier types of glasses, such as square frames of rectangle frames. A square face wearing square or rectangle glasses on will have the sharper and more intimidating features of the face too exaggerated, creating a less appealing profile.
  2. Select the frame size to fit the face: Face shapes vary greatly from person to person, as can face size. On an oval face, large and oversized glasses flatter the profile's features and draw attention to the perfect symmetry an oval face has. On a square face, that same size of frames may draw attention to a jawline that is too sharp, or make the face seem smaller than it actually is.
  3. Choose glasses that match a purpose: Polarised sunglasses may be perfect for outdoor activities or reducing the glare from the sun and the sun's reflection on any surface, but that same pair of glasses may not be suitable for a night event or an occasion that calls for a more professional appearance. The function and purpose for the glasses the wearer has should be decided on before purchasing a pair of designer glasses.
  4. Choose the material to suit purpose: Some materials used in the creation of designer glasses are much more delicate and easy to break than others. Acetate frame glasses are cost effective, able to fit polarised or anti glare coated lenses, and are durable and flexible in their design, making them perfect for fishing or other boat activities. A pair of rimless metal aviator sunglasses may be made of material that is easy to bend and break, making them ill suited to outdoor activity.
