<h1 class="title">Best Glasses for Pear Face (Male and Female): Frames, Shapes and Types</h1>

<p class="para-content">The best glasses for pear-shaped faces are round tortoiseshell, rectangle, round, cat-eye, oval and square glasses as they enhance their best features.</p>
<!--  -->
<p class="para-content">People with pear-shaped faces (triangle faces) use glasses to complement their narrow forehead and draw attention away from a fuller jawline, balancing their appearance. A pear-shaped face has a wider jaw, less prominent cheekbones and a narrow forehead. If you have a face shaped like a pear, glasses that are complementary include angular shapes that draw attention to the eyes and proportionate frames that balance out a broader jaw. For those with a pear face shape, glasses help to balance, highlight and enhance a person's best features, by adding breadth to the smaller elements of the upper face.</p>
<p class="intro para-content">Different types of glasses are used to complement people with pear-shaped faces, these glasses include <strong>round, tortoiseshell, rectangle, round, cat-eye, oval and square glasses.</strong> The best glasses for pear shaped faces and the reasons why are listed below.</p>
<ul class="unordered-list">
<li><span class="list-title">Round tortoiseshell glasses:</span> Tortoiseshell is a classic pattern that has remained one of the most popular colours for glasses. Round tortoiseshell glasses are a timeless design that complements pear face shapes by drawing attention to the upper parts of their face.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Rectangle glasses:</span> Rectangle glasses are a favoured frame design that is complementary to pear-shaped faces as they add a bold and striking quality to any face.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Round glasses:</span> The retro shape of round glasses are both a statement piece or an everyday pair that perfectly complements a pear face shape.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Cat-eye glasses:</span> The signature sharp and feline shape of a cat-eye frame helps to attract the eye upwards which is ideal for pear face shapes.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Oval glasses:</span> The soft curves of an oval frame suit a wide range of face shapes. For a pear shaped face, these glasses are ideal. From delicate metal rims to strong acetate frames, there is an oval glasses style for everyone.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Square glasses:</span> The classic square design always makes a strong statement for whoever is wearing them. The angular, square frame shape provides contrast to the softer features of a person with a pear-shaped face.</li>
</ul>

<p class="para-content">The round tortoiseshell glasses frame for pear faces, is a good choice of frame, as they are a<strong> modern classic design that makes a bold statement, suitable to be worn as an everyday pair.</strong> A round tortoiseshell frame is defined by its circular shape and intriguing pattern that is created using many different colours.</p>
<p class="para-content">The round tortoiseshell shape for pear faces is perfectly circular with smooth edges. It is suitable for pear faces because it softens the features of the face with its circular shape. The round tortoiseshell glasses change the proportion of a pear face by adding width and softness to the top of the face to contrast a larger, fuller jaw.</p>
<p class="para-content">Round tortoiseshell glasses add definition to the features and soften the eyes of a pear face. This frame is a timeless shape that has endured as a popular choice for different face shapes and is worn as both a casual style or dressed up for date night.</p>
<p class="para-content">The round tortoiseshell eyeglasses for a pear face are suitable for men, women and all genders as it is a neutrally flattering shape which makes them flexible for summer and winter wear. <h2 class="subtitle">How to Understand Whether you Have a Pear Face or Not?</h2>
<p class="para-content">To understand whether you have a pear face or not, you'll need to <strong>assess your facial characteristics and measure the dimensions. </strong>When measuring your face, there are four key measurements that determine your face shape. These measurements are the forehead, the cheekbones, the jawline and the length of the face from chin to hairline. If you have a pear face, your jawline is the widest part of your face, the face is longer than it is wide and the cheekbones and forehead is narrower than the jawline.</p>
<div class="flex-wrapper">
<div class="flex-left">
<p class="para-content">To understand whether you have a pear face you should follow the below instructions.</p>
<ol class="ordered-list">
<li>Measure the length: Use a measuring device such as a tape measure to estimate the length of your face from the centre of your hairline to the point of the chin.</li>
<li>Measure the width: Use the same tool to measure the width of the forehead. Do the same for the jawline and the cheekbones.</li>
</ol>
<p class="para-content">If all of these measurements starting at the forehead get progressively smaller as you move down the face, it could be an indication that you have a pear face.</p>
