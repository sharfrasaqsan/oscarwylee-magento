<h1 style="padding-top: 2em; text-align: left; font-size: 25px; font-weight: 600;">Single Vision</h1>
<div class="block-main-content" style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 1.7em; text-align: left; padding-bottom: 70px; margin-right: 20px;">These are lenses that have a single power over the whole lens. This means that they would be used by someone who needs glasses for driving or for reading. In most cases, a person’s eyes may need lenses to focus at both far and near distances and their prescription is the same for both. In this case, they can use the same single vision lenses for all activities.<br><br>Our <a href="/guides/lenses/single-vision.html">single vision distance lenses</a> are designed to help those who have trouble seeing things far away. If your prescription begins with a minus or if you’re nearsighted, this lens is for you. Single vision near lenses helps you to see things at a close distance of 30 to 40 cm, usually for activities such as reading or looking at a phone.</div>
