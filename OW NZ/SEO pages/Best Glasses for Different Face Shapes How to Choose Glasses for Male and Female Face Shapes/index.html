Which Glasses Type Suits Which Face Type?

There are many glasses types that suit people with certain face types. The main glasses types are rectangle, oval, square, cat-eye and round. The best glasses for each face type and the reasons why are listed below.

  • Rectangle glasses suit oval, round and diamond faces because their angular lines balance out the softer features of these face shapes.
  • Oval glasses suit square, diamond and pear faces as the soft curves offset their more angular features.

1. Round Face Shape

A round face shape is characterised by its circular and proportionate dimensions. A person with a round face will have fuller cheeks and a softly rounded chin. The proportions of a round face for men are different to women as men usually have a more square and angular chin whereas a women’s jawline is often softer and less defined. A round face is among the most common types of face shapes and is considered very flattering.

The best glasses for round faces are rectangle, square and geometric glasses as the angular lines help to offset the soft curves of this face shape.

If you have a round face shape, use your hairstyle to create interesting angles and elongate the face. The hairstyles that complement a round face are longer, soft waves, short hair with volume and asymmetric haircuts.

What are the Best Glasses for a Round Face Shape?

The best glasses for a round face shape are rectangle, square and geometric glasses. These glasses compliment round faces because they balance out the softer features and provide angles and strong lines.

What Glasses Types are not Compatible with a Round Face Shape?

The glasses types that are not compatible with round face shapes are glasses that highlight the circular shape instead of contrasting the roundness. These glasses include round, oval and aviator glasses, the reasons they are not good for round faces are listed below.

  • Round glasses: The spherical shape of round frames make a round face appear more circular, adding curves where glasses should be adding angles.
  • Oval glasses: Oval glasses are not recommended for round faces as they add to the fullness of the face shape, similar to round glasses.
  • Aviator glasses: Aviator frames do not complement a round face as they add curves and emphasise the fullness of the cheeks.
