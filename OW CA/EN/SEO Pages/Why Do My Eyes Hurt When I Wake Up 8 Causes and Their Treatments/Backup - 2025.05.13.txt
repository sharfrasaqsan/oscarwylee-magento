<h2 class="subtitle">How Common Is It For Eyes to Hurt When Waking Up?</h2>
<p class="para-content">Eye pain or your eyes hurting can be common <b>due to eye problems such as pink eye, dry eyes and eye strain which are all common eye problems and can occur suddenly.</b> Eye pain upon waking up can be normal as the lacrimal gland does not produce as many tears when you are sleeping. The lacrimal gland secretes fluids that lubricate, clean and protect the eyes. Tears help to retain the eye’s moisture, therefore, with fewer tears, you may experience pain in your eyes when waking up.</p>

<section class="square-section" id="causes">
<h2 class="subtitle">What are the Causes of Eye Pain When Waking Up?</h2>
<p class="para-content">The causes of eye pain when waking up can be a common symptom of many different conditions such as <b>pink eye, dry eyes, eye allergies, eye injury, angle-closure glaucoma, eyestrain, recurrent epithelial erosion and eyelid inflammation (blepharitis).</b> The causes of eye pain when waking up are listed below.</p>
<!-- ol ul -->
<ul class="ordered-list">
<li><span class="list-title">Pink Eye (Conjunctivitis):</span> Pink eye (conjunctivitis) is an infection or inflammation that can cause pain around the eye as well as redness and tearing.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Dry Eyes:</span> Dry eyes may be the cause of eye pain as the eyes require a good level of moisture to feel comfortable and maintain ocular eye health.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Eye Allergies:</span> Eye allergies can trigger pain in the eye as well as redness, burning, swelling and itchiness.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Eye Injury:</span> Eye injuries may likely induce pain due to the after effects of the injury and trauma to the eye.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Angle-Closure Glaucoma:</span> Angle-closure glaucoma may cause severe pain due to the increased pressure in the eye.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Eye Strain:</span> Eye strain can prompt eye pain as one of the symptoms due to the muscles in the eye developing fatigue from overexerting effort while focusing on certain tasks.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Recurrent Epithelial Erosion:</span> Recurrent epithelial erosion can be characterised as a sharp pain occurring upon waking up potentially due to the corneal epithelial cells unusually sticking to the outer layer of the cornea.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Eyelid Inflammation (Blepharitis):</span> Eye inflammation (blepharitis) is defined as an eyelid disease that can cause inflammation in the eyelid and can lead to pain in the cornea.</li>
</ul>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">1. Pink Eye (Conjunctivitis)</h3>
<p class="para-content">Pink eye (conjunctivitis) can cause pain in the eye <b>due to an infection or inflammation of the conjunctiva. Inflammation or infection of the conjunctiva can feel like there is debris stuck in the eye accompanied with watery eyes, itchiness and irritation.</b> It is possible to first notice pink eye when you wake up as you produce fewer tears while sleeping, which may exacerbate the symptoms. Pink eye (conjunctivitis) is defined as an infection or inflammation that can cause symptoms such as redness, tearing, itchiness and burning in the sclera (white part of the eye) and can develop due to a viral or bacterial infection or allergies.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">2. Dry Eyes</h3>
<p class="para-content">Dry eyes may be the cause of eye pain <b>due to the lack of moisture in the eyes from insufficient production and the poor quality of the tears that are being produced in the eye.</b> Eye pain when waking up is a common symptom of dry eyes as an adequate amount of tears is required to coat the eyes with enough moisture for optimal comfort and health. Dry eyes can also present symptoms such as stinging, itchiness, gritty or sandy sensation in the eye, redness and light sensitivity.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">3. Eye Allergies</h3>
<p class="para-content">Eye allergies can <b>commonly trigger eye pain as part of the many other symptoms that can occur during an allergic response.</b> Eye allergies caused by pollen and animal dander are likely causes of pain in one or both eyes when waking up. Other common symptoms of an eye allergy include eyelid swelling, redness and tearing.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">4. Eye Injury</h3>
<p class="para-content">Eye injuries may likely <b>induce pain from mild to severe depending on the nature of the initial cause. Different types of eye injuries can bring on pain as well as other serious symptoms,</b> therefore, it is important to seek medical attention if you are experiencing an eye injury. Common eye injuries can include a blow to the eye or a blunt force to the area around the eye, burns and irritation from chemicals or fumes, corneal abrasion, and a foreign object or debris going into the eye.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">5. Angle-Closure Glaucoma</h3>
<p class="para-content">Angle-closure glaucoma may cause eye pain <b>as a result of the increased pressure in the eye. Angle-closure glaucoma is a type of glaucoma that can develop when the fluid in the eye is hindered and cannot filter out</b>, causing a build-up of pressure. Symptoms of angle-closure glaucoma can include an abrupt onset of pain in one eye or a headache as well as nausea and vomiting. <b>Angle-closure glaucoma is considered a medical emergency, therefore, you will need to seek urgent assistance from a medical professional if you are experiencing these symptoms.</b></p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">6. Eye Strain</h3>
<p class="para-content">Eye strain can prompt eye pain when waking up <b>as a result of the eye muscles overexerting effort when focusing on certain tasks for long periods.</b> Waking up with eye strain could be a result of many different factors, however, it can likely occur from using the computer or your smartphone for long hours. Symptoms of eye strain can include tired, itchy or sore eyes according to Health Direct.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">7. Recurrent Epithelial Erosion</h3>
<p class="para-content">Recurrent epithelial erosion is <b>a syndrome that causes sharp pain in the eye when waking up along with blurred vision, photophobia and tearing.</b> A sharp pain is caused by the corneal epithelial cells unusually sticking to the inside of the upper eyelid as the surface of the eye dries. Recurrent epithelial erosion can occur as a result of corneal abrasion, corneal dystrophies, trauma to the cornea and diabetic patients according to the American Academy of Ophthalmology. If you are experiencing sudden eye pain, it is strongly recommended to seek urgent medical care.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">8. Eyelid Inflammation (Blepharitis)</h3>
<p class="para-content">Eyelid inflammation (blepharitis) is a disease that <b>occurs in the eyelid and can cause eye pain due to the swelling that occurs.</b> Blepharitis can occur in the form of anterior blepharitis, which can develop as a result of bacteria or dandruff from the scalp and eyebrows. Posterior blepharitis may occur from the abnormal production of oil which can allow bacteria to grow in the eyelid area according to the American Optometric Association. The symptoms of eyelid inflammation can include a burning or gritty sensation, itching, tearing, crusty discharge, redness and swelling in the eyelids.</p>
</div>

<h3 class="con-title">Is Age a Reason Why the Eyes Hurt When Waking Up?</h3>
<p class="para-content">Yes, age can be a reason why the eyes hurt when waking up <b>as age can increase the chances of developing eye conditions and problems that can induce pain.</b> Eye pain can occur as a result of certain health and genetic factors. However, dry eyes are a likely cause of eye pain in the morning among older patients as the body’s ability to generate the right amount of tears and adequate quality of tears can decline as we age.</p>
</div>
<div class="sub-div">
<h3 class="con-title">Can Sleeping Wrong Cause the Eyes to Hurt When Waking Up?</h3>
<p class="para-content">No, sleeping wrong does not cause the eye to hurt when waking up. <b>Sleeping incorrectly may not be a likely cause of eye pain as it is typically a symptom of an underlying problem or condition in the eye</b>, or is a result of a direct injury to the eye. However, sleeping wrong can lead to pain in your back, shoulders and neck depending on the position according to Orlando Health.</p>

<section class="square-section" id="treatments">
<h2 class="subtitle">What are the Treatments For Eye Pain When Waking Up?</h2>
<p class="para-content">The treatments for eye pain when waking up are <b>artificial tears, antibiotics, antihistamines and warm compress.</b> These are all effective treatment options that can help treat the initial eye condition that may be causing the eye to hurt after waking up. The treatments for eye pain when waking up are listed below.</p>
<!-- ol ul -->
<ul class="ordered-list">
<li><span class="list-title">Artificial Tears:</span> Artificial tears can be used to increase moisture in the eyes, which can help alleviate eye pain caused by dry eye. Using artificial tears for dry eye can take a few weeks to a month for effective treatment.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Antibiotics:</span> Antibiotics can help treat any eye infections that bring on pain due to inflammation and can come in the form of tablets or eye drops. Symptoms can start improving 3-4 days after starting antibiotics and are effective in treating eye infections.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Antihistamines:</span> Antihistamines can aid in relieving allergies which may be the cause of eye pain due to the swelling that typically occurs. Antihistamines can be taken as a tablet or used as eye drops and usually take effect after 30 minutes.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Warm Compress: </span> Warm compresses may be recommended to alleviate pain that can arise from certain eye conditions as it can improve the blood flow in the pain-inflicted areas. It also helps regulate oil production and helps prevent meibum blockages on the eyelid margins that can cause dry eyes.</li>
</ul>
<div class="sub-div" id="prevention">
<h3 class="con-title">What are Things to Do to Prevent Your Eye Hurting When Waking Up?</h3>
<p class="para-content">To prevent your eye from hurting when waking up, you can try <b>maintaining eye moisture, practising good hygiene, regularly taking anti-allergy medication</b> and staying up to date with <b>regular Eye exams.</b> The things to do to prevent your eye from hurting when waking up are listed below.</p>
<!-- ol ul -->
<ul class="ordered-list">
<li><span class="list-title">Maintaining eye moisture:</span> Maintaining eye moisture can help prevent the development of dry eyes and general eye pain and irritation. It may be helpful to instil artificial tears in your eyes right before going to sleep to reduce the chances of your eyes drying out during the night.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Practise good hygiene:</span> Practising good hygiene by washing your hands regularly and avoiding touching or scratching your eyes may lessen the likelihood of contracting any eye infections that can bring on pain.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Regularly take anti-allergy medication:</span> Regularly taking anti-allergy medication can help your allergies from progressing further which prevents swelling and pain in your eyes.</li>
<li><span class="list-title">Regular Eye exams: </span> Regular Eye exams can help with the early detection of any eye conditions that may trigger eye pain and can allow a quicker start on the prescribed treatment.</li>
</ul>
