Tips for wearing multifocal glasses

Adjusting to a brand new pair of multifocals can be difficult. But there are some useful tips that can ease the transition and help you settle into your glasses quicker.

  • Make sure to wear your new multifocals as much as possible to encourage your eyes to adapt to your new lenses.
  • Try not to go back to your previous glasses as it can affect the progress you have made with your new multifocals.
  • Move your head when looking around, not just your eyes. By pointing your nose where you want to see you will be looking out the clearest part of the lens.
  • If you are struggling to wear them initially, try using the new glasses in a comfortable and familiar environment to help build confidence.
  • If after 1-2 weeks of using your multifocals, you are still struggling to adjust, visit an Oscar Wylee store near you and our friendly team members will assist you.

Frame Styles

Acetate

Also known as cellulose acetate, these frames are traditionally hand-crafted by skilled craftsmen, with each frame passing through numerous stages of production and are hand-polished to ensure a glorious lustre. Our collections are a mix of Italian Mazzucchelli and Chinese-crafted plant-based plastic, which is both durable and light-weight.

Stainless-Steel

As part of our durable frame family, some of our most popular styles such as Kato, are now in stainless steel. These frames are hypoallergenic, corrosion resistant, light weight, and not sensitive to temperature changes.

Titanium

Our titanium frames are durable, corrosion resistant, lightweight, hypo-allergenic, with fixed spring hinges. Much of our titanium is sourced from Japan.

TR/Ultem material

We love our Ultem frames! They are super comfortable and very durable. Our Ultem frames are heat resistant, with high dielectric strength and are flexible. The material is widely used in medical and chemical instruments. It is currently available in a range of colours for both matte and gloss finish.
