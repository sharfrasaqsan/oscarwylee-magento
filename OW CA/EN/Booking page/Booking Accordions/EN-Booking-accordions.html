What Kind of Doctor Conducts Eye Exams?

An optometrist, also known as a doctor of optometry, conducts eye exams. An eye doctor in Canada is a licensed, board-certified healthcare professional who provides first point of contact eye care services.

Is an Optician Different from an Optometrist?

Yes, an optician is different from an optometrist due to their job responsibilities, education and training. Opticians are responsible for dispensing frames and lenses according to the prescription supplied by an optometrist or an ophthalmologist. You will likely see an optician working alongside an optometrist, but they will not have the same level of training or provide the same services. An optician will help fit frames for customers, give advice on the right lenses and manage the supply of glasses. They will not be able to conduct comprehensive eye exams like an optometrist, nor can they write prescriptions. According to the Opticians Association of Canada, an optician will have to undergo training at an accredited institution before they can begin practice. This can take from a year to 3 years, depending on the study load undertaken.

What Treatments Does an Optometrist Perform?

Optometrists are healthcare professionals who can perform treatments that may help correct refractive errors, double vision and treat eye infections by prescribing glasses and/or eye drops. The cost of the exam may be based on what your vision condition is and the nature of the necessary eye exams involved. The treatments optometrists can perform are listed below.

  • Refractive Errors: Refractive errors such as nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), presbyopia (age-related farsightedness) and astigmatism are common blurry vision problems that occur due to the shape of your eye preventing light from focusing on your retina. Optometrists can diagnose these conditions and prescribe glasses that may help correct them. If you are experiencing symptoms such as blurriness, eye strain or having to squint to see clearly, you can easily book an eye exam for refractive errors. If your vision does require corrective glasses, the cost of your exam may be affected as it may include other testing procedures and the price of your new prescription frames and lenses.
  • Double Vision: Double vision can be caused by a range of other problems or conditions, the optometrist will need to perform an eye exam and first determine what may be causing it in order to treat it. Optometrists can advise on proper fitting glasses or certain eye exercises to help. If the underlying problem is a more serious condition, you may be referred to an ophthalmologist. If the optometrist identifies signs of double vision, the overall cost may be affected as it will likely require additional testing processes for further assessment. You can call or talk to the staff in-store about booking an eye exam to check whether you have double vision or not.
  • Eye Infections: Eye infections can be minor issues and may only need antihistamine or antibacterial eye drops. If you are concerned about your symptoms, you can consult with your optometrist. Eye infections can easily be diagnosed with a regular eye exam. However, the cost may still depend on the severity of your condition and if the optometrist deems it necessary to conduct any other tests.

What are the Signs that Indicate a Need for an Immediate Eye Exam?

There are certain signs a person can look out for that indicate they should book an appointment for an immediate eye exam, including changes to your vision, such as blurry vision, seeing spots in your vision, pain in the eyes, if you have a foreign body in your eye and light sensitivity. However, it is always recommended that you see an optometrist when experiencing any other changes to your vision. The signs that indicate a need for an immediate eye exam are listed below.

  • Blurry Vision: Blurry vision may cause difficulty in seeing objects, people’s faces and shapes clearly or with sharpness. If you’re experiencing blurry vision, you may need prescription glasses and therefore will need to schedule an eye exam. If you already wear prescription glasses and experience blurry vision, this may indicate that you need to update your current prescription, which requires an eye exam.
  • Seeing Spots in Your Vision: Seeing spots in your vision may be associated with eye floaters as these spots may be parts of the vitreous that have clumped together, floating and drifting across a person's vision. It can be a sign that you need an immediate eye exam as it could indicate an underlying condition. However, while this symptom is not typically harmful and can be a natural sign of ageing, it is essential to address with an optometrist.
  • Pain in the Eyes: Pain in the eyes can be a sign of many different eye conditions and therefore, should be brought to the attention of an optometrist promptly. An eye exam can help determine the initial cause of this symptom and narrow down the most suitable treatment plan.
  • Foreign Body in your Eye: A foreign body in your eye may require the immediate attention of an optometrist or medical professional to determine the initial cause. The feeling of having something in your eye can stem from several different causes, making it crucial to have your eyes examined to rule out any major eye conditions.
  • Light Sensitivity: Light Sensitivity can be an unpleasant symptom of many different eye conditions, which is why it is important to get an immediate eye exam. This can help in identifying what may be causing this symptom and help determine what treatment may be needed.

Can Eye Exams Detect Other Issues Like Diabetes?

Yes, eye exams can detect other medical conditions such as diabetes and high blood pressure. According to Stanford University, a retinal examination can provide important information about a medical diagnosis, including high blood pressure and diabetes. A retinal examination is a type of test involving specialised tools like an ophthalmoscope, which looks at a patient’s retina and optic nerve.

No, there are no risks involved in having an eye exam. Eye exams are extremely important in maintaining eye health as well as your overall wellbeing. In the case of many eye diseases, early detection can be crucial as it can prevent vision loss, which is why regular eye exams are so important.
